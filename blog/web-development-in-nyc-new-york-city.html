Who Developed New York City?

New York City was developed over centuries, with contributions from various groups and individuals. Originally inhabited by Native American tribes such as the Lenape, the area began to be developed by European settlers in the early 17th century. The Dutch established the first permanent settlement, New Amsterdam, on the southern tip of Manhattan in 1624, which later became New York after the British took control in 1664. Over time, the city expanded through land reclamation, infrastructure projects, and the construction of iconic landmarks like the Brooklyn Bridge and skyscrapers during the industrial revolution and beyond.
