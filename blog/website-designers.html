Website Under Heavy Load

When a website is under heavy load, it means it is receiving an unusually high amount of traffic or requests that exceed its normal capacity. This can result in slow page loading times, crashes, or downtime. Websites that experience high traffic spikes due to viral content, marketing campaigns, or sudden events need to be optimized to handle such load efficiently. Solutions to manage heavy traffic include scaling up hosting resources, using Content Delivery Networks (CDNs), implementing caching techniques, and load balancing to distribute traffic evenly across servers.
