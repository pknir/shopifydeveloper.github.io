What is the Chicago Style Format?

The Chicago style format is a citation and formatting style commonly used in history, arts, and humanities. It offers two documentation systems:

Notes and Bibliography (NB) – used primarily in the humanities, requiring footnotes or endnotes along with a bibliography.
Author-Date – used in social sciences, with in-text citations and a reference list.

The format includes guidelines on page layout, margins, fonts, headings, and citations. It also specifies rules for citing various sources, including books, articles, websites, and multimedia.

Here's a general breakdown of what you can expect:

Small Business Websites ($1,500 - $5,000)
For basic websites with a few pages (e.g., home, about, services, contact), the cost typically ranges from $1,500 to $5,000. These websites are great for small businesses that need a professional online presence without complex features.
Mid-Range Websites ($5,000 - $15,000)
For businesses that need custom features, such as e-commerce functionality, booking systems, or interactive elements, the cost can range from $5,000 to $15,000. This includes a more personalized design and better user experience.
High-End Custom Websites ($15,000 - $50,000+)
Large corporations or businesses needing fully customized solutions with advanced functionalities like integrations with third-party tools, content management systems, and custom development may pay anywhere from $15,000 to $50,000 or more.
